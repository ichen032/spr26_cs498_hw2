For the average latency test over 10 iterations, the latencies are as follows:
us-central1:
Register avg (ms): 89.64
List avg (ms): 77.24

europe-west1: 
Register avg (ms): 542.28
List avg (ms): 434.72

For the eventual consistency experiment for Part IV-B, I got 0 misses for 100 attempts to register then retrieve a new user. 

The latencies differed significantly between the two VMs because of a difference in distance. As expected, requests made to the us-central1 VM had
much lower latency because it is much closer to my location and also the location of the database, which is also hosted in us-central1, than the
europe-west1 VM. The difference in network distance affects the time it takes for signals to travel and also affects whether intermediate hops are
necessary when sending information. 

I got 0 misses so my consistency experiment shows that data replication across regions is able to happen relatively quickly. We likely see this
result because the time difference between the register and list requests is enough for the update to propagate to the other replicas of the data.
Therefore, we are not able to detect any inconsistencies even when querying immediately after an update to a different region.
